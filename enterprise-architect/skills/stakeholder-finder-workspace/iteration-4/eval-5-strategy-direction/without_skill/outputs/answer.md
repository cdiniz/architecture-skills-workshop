# Payments Leadership Steering Session — Who Should Be in the Room

**Purpose framing:** This is a *strategic direction and mandate* session — setting multi-year priorities for payments. It is explicitly **not** a delivery, costing, or implementation exercise. The room should therefore be small and senior: people who set direction, own the payments mandate, hold the architecture vision, and carry the regulatory accountability. Hands-on delivery and squad-level engineering roles are intentionally excluded.

## Core attendees (decision-makers)

| Person | Role | Why they must be in the room |
|--------|------|------------------------------|
| **Aisha Khan** | Tribe Lead — Payments | Owns the payments tribe and its roadmap. She is the single person who aligns payments delivery direction with the platform strategy and regulatory deadlines. A strategic direction set without her cannot be executed. |
| **Bram Hendriks** | Domain Architect — Payments | Owns payments architecture standards and the *unified payments platform* vision. He frames the multi-year technical direction (event-driven processing, rail consolidation, resilience) that the strategy must rest on. Functional leader to the payments tribe architects. |
| **Mark van Dijk** | Architecture leader (functional/line leader across the architecture function) | Bram reports to him on the line, and he is the functional leader for integration, data, and cloud architects. He provides cross-domain architecture authority so payments direction stays coherent with the wider enterprise architecture. The person who can grant the architectural mandate. |
| **Jeroen Meijer** | Senior/Tribe leadership (Aisha's line manager) | Sits above the payments tribe in the line. Strategic priorities and multi-year mandate need this level of business/leadership ownership and sponsorship to stick. |

## Strongly recommended

| Person | Role | Why |
|--------|------|-----|
| **Miguel Santos** | Architecture leadership (functional leader for integration; Bram's functional/dotted-line leader) | Payments cannot be steered in isolation from integration and core-banking direction. Brings the cross-cutting integration mandate that any payments strategy depends on. |
| **Charlotte Dubois** (or her governance lead **Daan Verhoeven**) | Compliance Officer — Architecture | Payments is dominated by regulatory drivers (PSD2, instant payments). For a *multi-year mandate*, a regulatory/compliance voice belongs at the strategy table so the direction is set within the regulatory envelope, not retrofitted later. One compliance representative is enough. |

## Optional / consult, not necessarily in the room

- **Anika Sharma (Head of Data Architecture)** — relevant if data products / reporting / reconciliation data are expected to be a strategic pillar. Invite only if data strategy is on the agenda.
- **Ahmed Benali (Integration Architect)** and **Carlos Mendes (Cloud Architect)** — bring valuable input on the event backbone and cloud foundations, but these are delivery/enabling concerns. Brief them on the outcome; keep them out of the direction-setting room to preserve its strategic altitude.

## Deliberately excluded (delivery / execution layer)

To keep this a steering session and not a delivery review, the following are **not** invited: tribe squad members and engineers reporting into payments (e.g. Maria Oliveira, Jonas Bakker), and specialist/individual-contributor architects whose role is implementation rather than direction. They execute the mandate once it is set; they do not set it.

## Suggested room size

Keep it to roughly **4–6 people**: the four core decision-makers, plus the integration-architecture leader and a single compliance/governance representative. This preserves the strategic, mandate-setting nature of the session.
